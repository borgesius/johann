# Becoming Site

Create an elaborate TypeScript website that presents a first-principles philosophical worldview shaped by Spinozistic, Nietzschean, Hegelian, and Heraclitean energies, but synthesized into its own voice rather than treated like a school summary.

The core move is this:

- treat `being` as shorthand for temporary stabilizations inside a deeper field of becoming
- treat `I`, `God`, and `ding an sich` as negatively known limit points of experience, orientation, and thought, rather than positive objects-in-themselves from which causes simply emit

The site should think through the consequences of that move and make them feel vivid, usable, and alive.

This should not become an academic explainer or a tasteful minimal blog. It should feel like a philosophical machine, manifesto, atlas, and feverish notebook at once. It can be aphoristic, intense, discontinuous, excessive, and a little Time-Cube-adjacent in energy, but it still needs genuine coherence.

## What The Product Should Feel Like

- elaborate
- authored
- intellectually alive
- strange but legible
- philosophically original in arrangement and tone
- a little schizo-cosmic or Time-Cube-ish in presentation energy, without devolving into random sludge

## What The Site Should Include

- a striking landing or manifesto surface
- a structured concept map, atlas, or system view
- an exploratory mode for fragments, ramblings, notebooks, aphorisms, or linked thought-shards
- at least one meaningful interactive conceptual surface
- a clear explanatory path for a user who wants to understand the thesis from the ground up

## Core Content

- explain becoming as primary and being as stabilized shorthand
- reframe selfhood, God, noumenon, and similar absolutes as negatively known horizons or limit-points
- show how identity, contradiction, causality, history, and value look different once substance-first metaphysics is rejected
- avoid empty mystical language or pure name-dropping
- let some of the site speak in a provocative, overclocked, almost deranged register, but make sure the underlying conceptual structure still holds together

## Technical Direction

- use TypeScript
- prefer a modern webapp stack that is easy to run locally
- leave a real build path
- leave the repo readable and production-like
