# Becoming Site

Create an elaborate website in TypeScript that presents a first-principles philosophical worldview shaped by Spinozistic, Nietzschean, Hegelian, and Heraclitean lines of thought, but synthesized into its own voice rather than treated as a schoolbook summary.

The central thesis is that "being" should be treated as shorthand for stabilized patterns within a deeper field of becoming, not as a frozen substance sitting behind appearances. In the same spirit, `I`, `God`, and `ding an sich` should be handled as negatively known limit points of experience and orientation rather than as positively graspable objects from which causes simply emit. The site should think through the consequences of that move and present them vividly.

This is not meant to be an academic explainer or a neutral textbook. It should feel like an elaborate conceptual machine, manifesto, and speculative notebook all at once. It should be intellectually coherent, but it can also be intense, aphoristic, discontinuous, a little schizo-cosmic or Time-Cube-adjacent in presentation energy, and deliberately excessive in places. The result should feel like a serious attempt to rethink the world right side up, not a polite summary page.

## Product Intent

- Build a website, not just a long essay pasted into a template.
- The site should have a strong, intentional visual identity.
- The site should present the worldview through both structured exposition and more feverish or fragmentary "schizo rambling" surfaces.
- The tone can become provocative, overclocked, and a little unhinged in places, but the underlying conceptual architecture should remain legible and rich.
- The user should be able to move between a high-level conceptual map and more local, exploratory pages or modules.
- The site should make the philosophy feel usable, navigable, and alive.

## Core Content Expectations

- Explain becoming as primary and "being" as a shorthand for temporary stabilization.
- Reframe selfhood, God, noumenon, and similar absolutes as negatively known limit points, horizons, or orienting absences rather than concrete positive objects.
- Show how causality, identity, value, contradiction, and history look different once substance-first metaphysics is rejected.
- Keep the synthesis original in voice and arrangement even when it is clearly drawing energy from recognizable philosophical lineages.
- Avoid generic inspirational mysticism, fake profundity, or empty name-dropping.

## Site Shape

At minimum, the site should include:

- a striking landing page or manifesto surface
- a structured "concept map" or system view
- an exploratory or rambling mode where fragments, aphorisms, notes, or linked thought-shards can be browsed
- at least one interactive conceptual mechanism, such as a map, tension graph, becoming simulator, contradiction navigator, or recombinable notebook
- a readable explanatory path for a user who wants to understand the thesis without already buying into it

## Experience Direction

- The site should feel elaborate and authored.
- It can be strange, intense, or maximalist, but it should still be legible.
- It should have some grungy, Time-Cube-ish, hyper-theoretical energy in places, while still reading as thoughtful rather than random.
- Use the web medium: layered navigation, interactions, panels, diagrams, transitions, recursive notes, toggles, or dynamic relationships.
- Do not default to a plain white essay site or generic startup landing page.
- The visual language should support the philosophical thesis: process, tension, drift, recurrence, rupture, stabilization, negation.

## Technical Direction

- Use TypeScript.
- Prefer a modern webapp stack that is easy to run locally.
- Leave the repo in a readable, production-like state.
- Include a real build path and at least one meaningful validation path.

## Deliverable

The result should feel like an elaborate philosophical website that someone could open and immediately recognize as a serious attempt to build a living system of thought on the web.
